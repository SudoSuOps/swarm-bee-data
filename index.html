Cardiology — Acute Coronary Syndrome

Q: A 62-year-old male presents with substernal chest pressure radiating to the left arm for 45 minutes. ECG shows ST-elevation in leads II, III, and aVF. Troponin I is 2.4 ng/mL. BP 138/82, HR 88. What is the immediate management approach, and what are the key pharmacotherapy decisions in the first 24 hours?

A: This presentation is consistent with an inferior STEMI. Immediate management includes activating the cardiac catheterization lab for primary PCI (target door-to-balloon time <90 minutes). Administer aspirin 325mg chewable, ticagrelor 180mg loading dose (or clopidogrel 600mg if ticagrelor is contraindicated), and unfractionated heparin 60 U/kg bolus (max 4000 U). Sublingual nitroglycerin for ongoing pain, with caution given inferior MI (monitor for right ventricular involvement and hypotension). Morphine for refractory pain only. Post-PCI: dual antiplatelet therapy for 12 months, high-intensity statin (atorvastatin 80mg), beta-blocker (metoprolol tartrate 25mg BID titrated to HR goal), and ACE inhibitor (lisinopril 2.5–5mg daily) once hemodynamically stable. Echocardiography within 24–48 hours to assess LV function. This response is for educational purposes. All clinical decisions should be made by qualified healthcare providers based on individual patient assessment.

Frequently Asked

What is CoVe verification?

Chain-of-Verification is a three-step process from Meta AI research. For every claim in every answer, our pipeline: (1) Plans verification questions, (2) Executes those questions against a 235B parameter model, (3) Compares the verified answers with the original claims. If any claim fails, the pair is either rewritten with verified facts by the 235B model or rejected entirely.

What format is the data in?

JSONL (one JSON object per line). Each pair has: id, question, answer, specialty, source, and verification metadata. Every product includes the full dataset plus 80/10/10 train/val/test splits. Compatible with TRL, Axolotl, Unsloth, and any other fine-tuning framework.

Why is 79% of raw content rejected?

Small language models hallucinate. Especially in pharmacology — fabricated drug dosages, invented contraindications, wrong interaction mechanisms. Our CoVe pipeline catches all of it. The 21% that survives has every factual claim independently verified. That's what makes it platinum.

Can I use this commercially?

Yes. All products include a commercial use license. Fine-tune models, build products, deploy in production. Attribution appreciated but not required.

How is this different from open medical datasets?

Verification. Most medical QA datasets are generated and published without claim-level fact-checking. Ours runs 3 LLM calls per claim through a 235B verifier. The result is training data you can trust for clinical applications where hallucinated information is not just wrong — it's dangerous.
